Important Deadlines

Making sure that you remain aware and ahead of all relevant deadlines will be crucial for a successful college application experience

Timeline

December 7th (Junior Year)

The College Board Opportunity Scholarships Open

The College Board Opportunity Scholarships provide you with a chance to win money for college for doing things that you will need to complete anyway, such as build your college list or complete the FAFSA; thus, it is worth taking the time to look into.

August 1st (Senior Year)

The Common Application Opens

As the premiere method for students to easily apply to colleges, taking advantage of the Common Application will save you the hastle of having to apply to each school individually.

October 1st (Senior Year)

The FAFSA Opens

In order to earn federal financial aid, you must complete the FAFSA. Although the Fafsa doesn't close until over a year after this point, schools each have unique deadlines, and many require your financial aid to be submitted around the same time as your regular application. As a result of this, it is best to fill this out as soon as possible.

October-Novemeber (Senior Year)

Early Applications for Many Colleges Are Due

Applying early to colleges that offer an early action or early decision option can be a great way to not only alleviate some of the pressure that having multiples deadlines at once may pose but also to allow you to hear back from schools earlier

May 1st (Senior Year)

College Decision Day

Most colleges require students to decide whether or not they will be attending their school by this date.

June 20th (Senior Year)

Free AP Score Sends Must Be Used

As AP scores are a great means of garnering college credits prior to beginning college, ensuring that you are able to send your scores without charge will be important.
